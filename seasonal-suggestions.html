Seasonal Suggestions

Winter Suggestions


Winter is a tough time of year for growing vegetables, as conditions are harsh in most environments. Our biggest tip for any gardener is to use your winter period for pruning and prepping your garden or growing area. Making sure that your area is ready for spring will be crucial to getting a great first harvest planted. If you're still keen on having something in the ground, why not try some robust root vegetables such as:


  • Potatoes

  • Garlic

Spring Suggestions


As spring begins, so does our gardening cycle! As the days grow longer and warmer, spring offers a magical mix of sunshine and rain to create the ideal conditions for plant growth. This time of year, those pesky pests and diseases are also less active, giving your crop the best chance of survival! While there is an abundance of choice, why not try some of these to get your started:


  • Herbs

  • Seeds

  • Peas

  • Tomatoes

Summer Suggestions


The summer has seen our first harvest from our spring crop, and offers an ideal opportunity for us to plant for a late season harvest too. The warm weather can make it tough for some veggies to germinate, but there are a few that thrive in the summer heat. Why not try your hand at some of these for a challenge:


  • Beans

  • Swiss Chard

  • Cucumbers

  • Lettuce

Autumn Suggestions


As autumn creeps in, there's still plenty to do before the winter. Harvesting the last of our summer crop leads us into our cleanup and breakdown of our summer patch. This is an important step to get us ready to prep and prune the space without allowing too much to grow freely during the winter months. Before we take out seedlings inside for the winter, why not give these a go for spooky season:


  • Pumpkins

  • Turnips

Today's Top Tip


Planting too early (or late) in the season can spell disaster for your garden. You need to know the last average spring frost date for your area so you don't accidentally kill plants by putting them out prematurely. It's also good to know your first average fall frost date so that you get your plants harvested or moved indoors before late-season cold damages them. Discover the average first and last frost dates for your area.
