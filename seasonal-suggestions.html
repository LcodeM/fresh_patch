Winter Suggestions


Winter is a tough time of year for growing vegetables, as conditions are harsh in most environments. Our biggest tip for any gardener is to use your winter period for pruning and prepping your garden or growing area. Making sure that your area is ready for spring will be crucial to getting a great first harvest planted. If you're still keen on having something in the ground, why not try some robust root vegetables such as:


  • Potatoes

  • Garlic

Today's Top Tip


Planting too early (or late) in the season can spell disaster for your garden. You need to know the last average spring frost date for your area so you don't accidentally kill plants by putting them out prematurely. It's also good to know your first average fall frost date so that you get your plants harvested or moved indoors before late-season cold damages them. Discover the average first and last frost dates for your area.
